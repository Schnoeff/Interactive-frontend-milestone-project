Brighton

A little bit about Brighton

Londoners have been travelling to Brighton for beach getaways ever since the railway arrived here in 1841. The pebbled beach, Brighton Pier's amusement arcade and the Royal Pavilion are the main sights, but you'll also find hundreds of pubs and clubs catering to an energetic crowd.

A room with a view

Choose from a selection of boutique sea view rooms from a rooftop balcony room to deluxe rooms with roll top baths and monsoon showers. Enjoying a premium spot opposite the beach and close to the Palace Pier makes this an ideal location for a stay in Brighton. In an area known as Kemp Town many things are on your doorstep, with Brighton city centre only a 10 minute stroll away. A key feature of this property is that it is lucky enough to be on the sea front and also on the corner of an attractive garden square giving rise to its quirky and yet rather old school name.

Sea Spray Boutique Hotel

If you’re looking for a trendy bed and breakfast in Brighton, look no further than Sea Spray Boutique Hotel. For those interested in checking out popular landmarks while visiting Brighton, Sea Spray Boutique Hotel is located a short distance from Kemptown (0.5 mi) and Saint Paul’s Parish Church (0.6 mi). Guest rooms offer a flat screen TV, and Sea Spray Boutique Hotel makes getting online easy as free wifi is available. You can also take advantage of some of the amenities offered by the bed and breakfast, including room service, express check-in and check-out, and baggage storage. In addition, guests can enjoy breakfast buffet during their visit. Reception is attended between 8am to 9pm. Should time allow, The Lanes, Royal Pavilion, and Brighton Palace Pier are some popular attractions that are within walking distance

Artist Residence Brighton

If you could condense the spirit of a town into one building, Artist Residence Brighton would be it. Creative, bohemian and downright eccentric, this unique boutique hotel in a townhouse at the head of Regency Square was reborn when we sent out an ad offering artists free board in return for their services. As a result, each of the 24 rooms is a riot of quirky original artwork and local character, with eye-popping sea views over Brighton seafront and the iconic West Pier. Guests can dine out at Dan Kenny's refined 20-cover restaurant The Set and enjoy tapas and draught craft beers in the buzzing Set Bar.

  • Wifi
  • Good location
  • Bar/Lounge
  • Games room

Price: From £80 per night
